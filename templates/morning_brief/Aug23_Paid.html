ANALYSIS: Canada Is On Track for Record Asylum Claims This Year—Here’s Why
Subscriber-only  

After years of asylum seekers using a loophole and entering Canada across unofficial border crossings, Ottawa and the U.S. have closed that door.
But the number of asylum seekers continues to rise despite this measure. Epoch Times' Tara MacIsaac looks at the reasons behind it.
Why It Matters: Canada processed 92,000 asylum claimants in the first quarter of this year, magnitudes larger than last year's record-breaking 57,000.

How the Rail Stoppage May Impact Food Costs and Supply
Open Access  

Although the government said it will force binding arbitration to end the rail shutdown, the labour minister says it could be days before trains will be moving again.
Epoch Times’ Matthew Horwood explores how the disruption could impact food costs and availability in grocery stores.
Why It Matters: Canada is heavily reliant on rail transportation, with CN and CPKC hauling a combined $1 billion in goods per day.
